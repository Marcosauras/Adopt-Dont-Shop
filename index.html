why Adopt don't shop

Adopt, Don't Shop is a campaign slogan that a growing number of animal rights proponents are using to promote adopting pets from shelters, rather than buying them from pet stores. Many of the animals sold in pet stores or online originate from puppy mills, commercial animal-breeding facilities that focus on increasing profit with little regard for the health and welfare of the animals. Although puppy mills in the U.S. are legal, a vast majority aren't regulated. Practices and conditions in most puppy mills are unethical and downright abhorrent in many cases. Dogs live in filthy conditions without adequate food, water, or veterinary care. And female dogs are bred at every opportunity with little to no recovery time between litters. Inbreeding is also a huge problem.

Reasons to adopt from a shelter

  • You're Saving A Life

    Adopting an animal from a shelter not only means that you're giving them a happy life, but you also free up a spot at the rescue or shelter to save another animal's life.

  • You Can Find A Trained Animal

    If you don't know much about training an animal or don't have the patience? You can find an adult rescue animal who's already house trained and has his basic manners down pat.

  • Rescue Animals Are Often Less Expensive To Adopt

    Rescue animals are much cheaper to adopt, having low adoption fees and most of them have been spayed or neutered.

  • Helps Fight Puppy Mills

    Approximately 90% of puppies you can buy in pet stores or online are from puppy mills. Adopting a dog from a shelter takes business away from mills. The more people who adopt, the more puppy mills have a hard time staying in business.
